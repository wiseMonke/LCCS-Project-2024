Primary Research I conducted

  • I created a survey on Microsoft Forms to gather data on potential end users for my product.
  • I sent every 6th year in my school the survey and collected the response on excel and sorted them out into different sheets. I also graphed the data, using the graph function on excel as this allows me to read the data more easily and interpret it quicker.
  • A problem I faced was that, not everyone responded to my survey, I only got 30 students out of my whole year (110 students) answering my survey in the whole week.
  • I also was aware of the fact, that my data and demographic interest is heavily skewed and biassed and not representative of the whole population. This is because I only asked 17-18 year old, male teenagers, who are in school (and only 25% of those answered). (If I had more time and more resources, I would have picked a better sample population, consisting of people from every demographic to get the most accurate demographic data about the population)
  • My main goals from doing the forms, was to figure out the INTEREST, DEMAND, USEFULNESS, DEMOGRAPHICS, USER-NEEDS and FEATURES

User Needs

  • Users want a highly customizable product, which they can make their own and add their own vibe.
  • Users want a highly portable product, which can run everywhere and must have a good battery life, as they hate having to charge it regularly
  • Users ideally are looking for a cheaper alternative to current fitness trackers on the market.
  • Users also are very concerned about their privacy and biometric data.This means, we needs to store data safely.
  • The Users also wanted the product to be accurate. They saw no reason to switch from industry leaders, unless my product was more accurate.

Existing Products and Solutions

  • Currently, there are a couple of products that partially fulfil the features of my product. Examples are (Apple Watch, Samsung Watch, FitBit & Garmin trackers). These are the industry standard and leaders in this market.
  • There are many fitness trackers that accurately track sleep and stress. These trackers use external sensors to do so.
  • There currently are no commercial products, that combine all three factors (sleep, stress & light levels) and determine your happiness level via a video.
  • My product has a downside as it does not measure/track the above factors but instead relies on User Input about their satisfaction of the three factors (sleep, stress & light levels) to bias the ML algorithm to show the (%) happiness (maybe less accurate).

Features of my product and its USP (Unique Selling Point)

  • It is highly customisable and you can always add more sensors and trackers to the Raspberry Pi in the future.
  • It is open-source as all the code is available to read and you can customise it to your wish.
  • It is much cheaper than the other solutions in the market ($40, $30 for Rpi & $10 for camera module).
  • Offers data security as the data is stored locally.
